# Usability

Source: https://learn.microsoft.com/zh-tw/windows/apps/design/usability/

Source snapshot: Microsoft Learn zh-tw page reviewed on 2026-06-06.

Inclusive design is part of the product surface.

## Guidelines

- Accessibility: support users who cannot or prefer not to use a traditional UI. Verify keyboarding, focus order, names, roles, contrast, narrator/screen-reader behavior, and text scaling.
- App settings: provide settings that let users adapt the experience, then persist them correctly.
- Globalization/localization: design for different languages, regions, formats, cultures, text lengths, and bidirectional layouts.
- In-app help: provide help when needed, but show it on demand most of the time.
- Instructional UI: use it to reveal non-obvious gestures or features, not to explain basic UI that should be self-evident.
